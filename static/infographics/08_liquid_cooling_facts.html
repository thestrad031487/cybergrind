AIO vs CUSTOM LOOP
AIO (ALL-IN-ONE)
Pre-filled sealed loop. Waterblock, pump, tubing, and radiator in one unit. Easy install, no maintenance.
PLUG-AND-PLAY
CUSTOM LOOP
DIY: separate pump, reservoir, blocks, radiator, tubing. Fully configurable. Can cool CPU + GPU + RAM.
MAXIMUM PERFORMANCE
▸ AIO radiator sizes: 120mm / 240mm / 280mm / 360mm
▸ Larger radiator = more surface area = lower temps
▸ Custom loops use distilled water + biocide additive
▸ Glycol (antifreeze) prevents corrosion in mixed-metal loops
IMMERSION COOLING
SINGLE-PHASE
Components submerged in dielectric fluid (mineral oil or engineered fluid). Fluid pumped to external heat exchanger. No phase change. Messy maintenance.
TWO-PHASE
Fluid boils at CPU operating temp. Vapor rises, condenses on cooled coil, drips back. Highly efficient but expensive. No moving parts allowed in system.
  • PUE of 1.05 vs 1.35 for air cooling (Wikipedia, Computer Cooling)
  • Handles up to 100 kW per rack vs ~23 kW for air
  • Primarily used in data centers, not consumer builds
  • System cannot have spinning drives (e.g., HDDs)
ACTIVE-LIQUID HEAT SINK (ALHS)
  • Self-contained: liquid heat sink + internal pump + fan + radiator in one unit
  • No external liquid pumps required
  • More compact than full liquid cooling loops
  • Consistently outperforms air-cooled and standard heat pipe systems
  • Best thermal performance of any single-cooler solution
COOLING HIERARCHY: AIR < WATER / AIO < ALHS < IMMERSION

HEAT SINK — HOW IT WORKS
HEAT TRANSFER PATH: CPU → IHS → THERMAL PASTE → BASE → FINS → AIR → CASE EXHAUST
FAN CONFIGURATIONS
🔁 SERIES (STACKED)
Fans stacked top-to-top. Increases static pressure. Better for dense fin stacks.
⇔ PARALLEL (SIDE-BY-SIDE)
Fans side-by-side. Increases airflow volume. Better for open fin designs.
PROS & CONS
✓ ADVANTAGES
Most affordable cooling option

Very low maintenance needs

✗ LIMITATIONS
Less efficient at extreme heat

HEAT SINK TYPES
  • Tower Cooler — Tall upright fins with side-mounted fans. Excellent thermal mass, suits full towers.
  • Low-Profile / Slim — Flat design for ITX/SFF builds. Trades performance for clearance.
  • Top-Down Blow — Fan blows directly down onto fins and motherboard VRMs. Good for low-clearance cases.
  • Dual Tower — Two stacked fin arrays with fans between. Maximum air cooling performance.
  • Heat Spreader (laptop) — No fins or fans. Spreads heat across a flat surface. Common in slim laptops.
THERMAL PERFORMANCE FACTORS
▸ Air velocity — higher CFM = more heat removed
▸ Fin material — copper > aluminum for conductivity
▸ Fin design — flared fins reduce airflow resistance
▸ PWM control — fan speed adjusts to CPU temp
▸ Heat pipes — copper vapor chambers accelerate heat transfer
▸ Thermal paste — fills microscopic gaps at CPU-HS interface
▸ TDP rating — cooler must exceed CPU's thermal design power
▸ Ambient temp — room temperature limits minimum CPU temp

NIST Frameworks · Visual Reference

CSF 2.0 & SP 800-53

From executive order to control catalog — how the two most important NIST cybersecurity frameworks were built, what they do, and how they work together.

01 Origin Story
2002
FISMA Enacted
Federal Information Security Management Act directs NIST to develop security standards for federal information systems.
2005
SP 800-53 v1 Published
First version released as part of FISMA Implementation Project alongside FIPS 199 & 200.
2013 / 2020
Rev. 4 → Rev. 5
Revision 5 (Sept. 2020) expands scope beyond federal agencies, integrates privacy controls, adds Supply Chain & PII families.
2013
Executive Order 13636
President Obama orders NIST to develop a cybersecurity framework to protect critical infrastructure.
2014 / 2018
CSF 1.0 → CSF 1.1
Initial release targets critical infrastructure sectors. Rapidly adopted by public and private organizations worldwide.
Feb 26, 2024
CSF 2.0 Released
Expanded scope to all organizations. Added Govern as a sixth core function. Stronger supply chain & governance guidance.

04 SP 800-53 — Security Baselines
Low Impact
~149
Controls Required
For systems where a breach would have limited adverse effects on operations, assets, or individuals. A starting foundation.
Moderate Impact
~287
Controls Required
For systems where a breach would have serious adverse effects. The most commonly applied baseline across federal agencies.
High Impact
~370
Controls Required
For systems where a breach could cause severe or catastrophic harm — national security, critical infrastructure, or public safety.

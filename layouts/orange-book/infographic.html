General Computing
Supercomputers
1890
Hollerith's Tabulating Machine
Herman Hollerith builds an electromechanical punch-card machine to process the US census. Completes it in 2.5 years instead of 14. His company later becomes IBM.
7 years → 2.5 years census time
1944
Harvard Mark I
Harvard & IBM complete the world's most powerful computer. Used in WWII and the Manhattan Project. Weighs 5 tons, 50 feet long, ~750,000 parts.
3 additions/sec · 1 multiply / 6 sec
1954
NORC — IBM's first supercomputer
Built for the US Navy. Calculated pi to 3,089 digits in 13 minutes at its 1954 dedication. World's fastest computer until 1963.
$2.5M · 2,000-word memory
1947
The Transistor
Bell Labs scientists invent the transistor — replacing slow mechanical relays with silicon. Smaller, faster, cheaper, and no moving parts. The foundation of all modern electronics.
1959
The Microchip
Texas Instruments invents the integrated circuit — fitting an entire computer circuit onto a single board. Costs fall. Personal computing becomes possible.
1965 – 69
CDC 6600 & 7600 — Seymour Cray
CDC 6600 executes 3M instructions/sec — 10× faster than anything else. Its successor, the 7600, uses pipelining to reach 10× that. Fastest in the world 1969–75.
100+ miles of wiring in CDC 6600
1972 – 75
ILLIAC IV
First computer with solid-state memory. In 1975, became the first network-available supercomputer — connected to ARPANET, the internet's precursor.
1975
CRAY-1 — fastest in the world
Seymour Cray's first machine from his own lab. 160 megaflops, 8 MB memory. First unit installed at Los Alamos for $8.8M. World record until 1981.
1977
The PC Revolution
Apple II, TRS-80, and Commodore PET launch — the "1977 trinity." Also: Atari 2600. For the first time computers are marketed to ordinary consumers.
1991 – 99
Cray C90, T3E & TOP500 era
Cray C90 first to hit 1 gigaflop (1991). The TOP500 ranking list launches in 1993. Cray T3E-1200E first to sustain 1 teraflop on a real application (1999).
Gigaflop → Teraflop in one decade
1994
World Wide Web goes public
The web opens to the public. Email, chat, and early social media transform PCs from productivity tools into communication platforms. PC ownership skyrockets.
2007
The Smartphone
Apple unveils the iPhone — a pocket-sized internet computer. Smartphones rapidly replace personal landlines. Most global internet traffic is now mobile.
2022 World record
Frontier — first exascale computer
HPE + AMD + Oak Ridge break the exascale barrier. Frontier achieves 1.102 exaflops — faster than the next 7 supercomputers combined.

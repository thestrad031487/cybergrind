## From the Trenches

As a cybersecurity practitioner, I'm constantly on the lookout for emerging threats that can compromise our most critical systems. Two recent developments have caught my attention and warrant serious consideration from IT teams everywhere.

The growing integration of AI in various industries has led to new security risks, particularly in healthcare (Forbes). The increased reliance on AI-powered systems means there are more potential entry points for attackers, who can exploit vulnerabilities in these systems to gain unauthorized access. For instance, a recent vulnerability in Veeam backup servers allows attackers to execute remote code execution attacks (BleepingComputer), which is a serious concern for organizations that rely on these systems to protect their data.

Another story that has piqued my interest is the launch of BlueVoyant AI-powered cloud detection and response capabilities, which aims to advance cyber defense in the post-Mythos era (Cisco.com). As more organizations move their operations to the cloud, it's essential to have robust security measures in place to prevent data breaches. By leveraging AI-powered solutions like BlueVoyant, organizations can improve their visibility into cloud-based threats and respond more effectively.

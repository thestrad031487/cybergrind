## From the Trenches

As a cybersecurity practitioner, I've been keeping an eye on the latest developments in the world of industrial cybersecurity and AI-powered threats. Two stories that caught my attention are Rockwell Automation's announcement of Luminus Selects SecureOT Platform to support industrial cybersecurity resilience, and the Qilin Ransomware attackers' exploitation of PAN-OS Authentication Bypass for initial access.

The adoption of SecureOT Platform by Rockwell Automation is a significant move towards improving the resilience of industrial cybersecurity. This platform provides a comprehensive solution for managing and mitigating cyber threats in industrial control systems, which are often critical infrastructure components that require robust security measures. As the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) continues to grow, it's essential that organizations like Rockwell Automation prioritize their cybersecurity posture.

On the other hand, the Qilin Ransomware attackers' exploitation of PAN-OS Authentication Bypass for initial access highlights the importance of patching and vulnerability management in industrial environments. This vulnerability was previously disclosed as CVE-2026-0257, but its active exploitation by ransomware groups underscores the need for organizations to prioritize remediation efforts.

**🔧 Patch Priority:** Google's Gemini software should be patched immediately due to its potential exposure to unauthorized access via a bug that can be exploited by malicious actors.
